Clergy

Fr. Habib Ghantous

Fr. Habib Ghantous has served the Antiochian Orthodox Christian Church with devotion, humility, and pastoral love for nearly two decades. Ordained to the Holy Priesthood in 2005, Father Habib spent many years offering liturgical services, preaching and ministering to the faithful during weekends and evenings while simultaneously working full-time in the corporate sector. In 2025, he answered the calling to serve full-time in parish ministry at St. George Orthodox Church in Terre Haute, Indiana.

His ministry is rooted in humility, compassion, and a deep commitment to the spiritual well-being of the faithful. Fr. Habib is known for his heartfelt preaching, his pastoral presence during times of joy and sorrow, and his desire to help every person grow closer to Christ. He is especially dedicated to strengthening parish life through teaching the Faith, visiting the sick and homebound, guiding families, and fostering unity within the community.

Before entering full-time ministry, Fr. Habib spent more than three decades in program and project management, leading international teams and developing skills in organization, service, and mentorship. These experiences now support his ministry, helping him guide the parish with clarity, steadiness, and care.

Fr. Habib is a devoted husband to Khourieh Norma and a proud father to three sons. He is grateful for the privilege of serving the people of St. George and walking with them on the path of faith, hope, and love in Christ.

Rev. Archdeacon Elias Corey

Rev. Archdeacon Elias Corey was born in Terre Haute, Indiana to Alexander and Jamily (Haddad/Shaheen) Corey. Lou or Louis as he is known in his secular life, married Denise Marie Deacon of Parma, Ohio. They met at a Parish Life Conference held in Akron, Ohio in 1977.

He continued his education at Indiana State University and later operated retail business in Terre Haute, ultimately opening Mister Corey’s Fine Footwear. He sang as a life member of the St. George Choir until he was asked to assist at the chanter’s stand and was guided toward ordination.

In 1986, Lou was ordained to the Holy Diaconate by His Eminence, Archbishop MICHAEL (Shaheen) of Toledo, Ohio. On Bright Thursday, April 12, 2018, he was elevated to the rank of Protodeacon by His Grace, Bishop ANTHONY of Toledo during a hierarchal visit, honoring his decades of dedicated service.
